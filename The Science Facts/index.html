1.

The speed of light in a vacuum is approximately 299,792,458 meters per second, making it the fastest known phenomenon in the universe.

2.

The Earth is not a perfect sphere, but rather is an oblate spheroid, meaning it is slightly flattened at the poles and bulges at the equator.

3.

The Earth's atmosphere is made up of 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, and trace amounts of other gases, including carbon dioxide and water vapor.

4.

The Earth is not the only planet in the solar system with liquid water on its surface. Scientists have found evidence of liquid water on Mars and several of the moons in our solar system, including Europa and Enceladus.

5.

The average temperature on Earth is about 15°C (59°F), but the temperature can vary greatly depending on location and time of year.

6.

The Earth's magnetic field protects the planet from harmful solar radiation, and helps to create the auroras that are visible in the polar regions.

7.

The oceans on Earth contain about 97% of the planet's water, and the remaining 3% is found in lakes, rivers, and the atmosphere.

8.

The Earth's rotation causes the sun to rise in the east and set in the west, and also causes the change in seasons.
